Excerpt

Age: 3

BREAKING NEWS!

Toddler Suffers Serious Injury!

This just in from the Jiang household: David Jiang has tripped and skinned his knee!

It was a beautiful spring day: perfect for playing outside. But this serene scene was forever ruined when David tripped and skinned his knee. Experts have reached the consensus that a combination of him wearing shorts and tripping while running on asphalt is to blame for the extent of the wound. Testimony from on-site paramedic "Mom" has revealed that the skin on his right knee has been almost completely rubbed off, and that the area is bleeding profusely.

One of our correspondents reached out to David for comment on the matter. He had this to say:

"My elbow-leg!"

David then went back to crying, and no further comments were issued.

Piaget's Commentary

In the preoperational stage, children begin to use language to express their schemas. As evidenced by him not knowing the word "knee," this child is still in the preoperational stage, as he does not have complete mastery of the English language.
What he did say is evidence of assimilation. Not knowing the word for "knee," he drew from his existing schema of human anatomy, describing his knee as his leg's elbow.
